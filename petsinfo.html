Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: What kind of dog should I adopt?

A1: Find as much information about the dog you are interested in adopting, click here to find more info from the shelter you want to adopt from.

Q2: What supplies do I need to have before I own a dog?

A2: After you have decided what type of dog to own, some basic supplies include:

    • Leash and Collar
      ID Tag, all current information
      Dog friendly toys
      Dog food fit for your pet
      Dog bedding
      Water and food bowls, preferrably stainless steel

    • For detailed information checkout the Resources Page.

    Q3: Which vaccines does my new dog need?

    A3: Make sure your dog has at least the following vaccinations:

    • rabies virus
      canine distemper virus
      canine adenovirus-2(hepatitis)
      canine parvovirus

    • For more information about the vaccines click on the Resources Page.

    Q4: Which vet clinic should I visit with my new dog?

    A4: There are many factors to consider when choosing a vet clinic. There are shelters that offer clinic services and are non-profit, as well as for-profit clinics; depending on the needs of your dog and your budget, might want to gather more information from the shelter you adopted your dog from, or just Click Here to find a vet near your area for more information.

    Q5 What type of questions should I ask the shelter about the dog I choose?

    A5: As a new pet owner of the dog you choose, you might want to ask key questions to get an idea if the dog you found near your area is the right fit for you. Some questions include:

    • How long has the pet been in this shelter?
      Has this pet been adopted before? If yes, why was it returned?
      Are there any behavioral issues I should be aware of before I adopt it?
      Does this dog have any training?
      If the dog cannot adapt to its new home, can I return it to the shelter?

    • Q6: I am an experienced dog owner, can I add resources or tips to help adopters in this site?

    A6: Most certainly! Hop on over to our Resources Page, to add to our community forum!
